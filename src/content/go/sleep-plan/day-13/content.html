Protecting Your Sleepiness Like It's Precious

You're on day 13 of your 30-day sleep journey ✨ and honestly? You're in the thick of it now. You've been practicing the 15-minute rule (getting out of bed when sleep won't come), and today we're adding a layer that makes all the difference: how you move when you get up matters just as much as that you get up.

Think of sleepiness as this delicate, flickering thing. You know that feeling when you're right on the edge of sleep, that lovely heavy feeling in your eyelids? When you have to get out of bed in the middle of the night, your job is to protect that feeling like you're carrying a candle through a dark room. One bright light, one sudden movement, and poof, it's gone.

The "sleepy walk" isn't about shuffling around like a zombie (though honestly, if that's what it looks like, you're doing it right). It's about staying in the sleep zone even when you're technically awake. It's about moving through your space like you're still part of the dream world, not fully rejoining the waking one.

Why This Matters

Here's what happens when you flip on a bright overhead light or start scrolling your phone in the middle of the night: your brain gets a wake-up signal. Blue light especially tells your brain "it's morning! time to be alert!" and boom, that precious drowsiness you were holding onto is gone. Your heart rate picks up, your thoughts start racing, and suddenly getting back to sleep feels impossible.

But when you keep things dim and move slowly, you're telling your brain "we're still in sleep mode, just taking a brief intermission." Your nervous system stays calm. Your melatonin levels don't crash. That heavy, sleepy feeling? It hangs around, waiting for you to crawl back into bed.

This isn't about making things inconvenient or stumbling around in the dark (please don't hurt yourself!). It's about being intentional. You're not "up" in the middle of the night. You're just… elsewhere for a moment. Still in your cocoon of sleepiness, just in a different room.

How to Make It Stick

Set up your sleep-friendly lighting now

Before bed tonight, put a small nightlight in your hallway or the room where you'll go if you can't sleep. Choose something with warm amber or red tones if possible (not blue or bright white). Position it low, near the floor, so it lights your path without blasting your eyes.

Practice slow-motion movement

When you get out of bed tonight (if you need to), imagine you're moving through water. No sudden gestures, no quick steps. Keep your shoulders soft, your breathing steady. You're not "getting up" really you're just relocating your resting body to another spot.

Keep your eyes soft

Don't stare at light sources. Let your gaze stay a little unfocused, dreamy. If you need to read, hold the book farther away than usual and keep the light dim. You're not trying to wake up enough to be productive. You're staying just awake enough to not be in bed.

Tonight's Gentle Reminder

If you find yourself needing to get out of bed tonight, remember: you're protecting something delicate. Move like you're still dreaming. Keep the lights low. Stay soft in your body. You're not abandoning sleep; you're just giving it a little space to find you again.

You're getting really good at this, you know. Week 2 is almost done, and tomorrow (Day 14) we'll pause to look at everything you've practiced this week. But tonight? Just focus on the walk. Slow. Dim. Dreamy.

Rest easy 🌙
